01 // History & Mission

Our Mission & Timeline

"It takes an entire village to raise a child." The LaSalle Educational Club, Inc. acts as both an educational trampoline and a foundational community safety net.

"To provide healthy, social, and wholesome childhood experiences for youth, empower families to be independent and resourceful, and ensure educational success by raising college scholarship funds."

1957 — Ever-Ready Mothers Club

Founded by Mrs. Lexie M. Sanders and 25 visionary mothers operating out of the Griffon Manor housing complex to establish safe, constructive youth community footprints.

1967 — First Awards Banquet

Inaugurated the formal annual Awards Banquet framework to honor local high school graduation metrics.

1979 — The First Formal Scholarship

Formally introduced and granted the historic Rev. James M. Bradley Scholarship to support higher education paths.

1989 — The LaSalle Educational Club

Renamed to explicitly center the organization's overarching modern imperative: full-scale academic support and college funding.

04 // Requirements Portal

Application Guidelines

Students of all ethnic backgrounds, military enrollees, and incoming vocational or trade school assets are explicitly encouraged to apply.

Submission Inventory Check

  • [01] Official High School Transcript directly submitted.
  • [02] Formal Cover Letter precisely identifying target awards.
  • [03] A structured, individual 500-word academic essay.
⚠️ Mandatory Rule

Banquet Attendance

Award winners must be physically present at the ceremony. If absent, a parent or legal guardian must attend to receive the award on their behalf, or it will be forfeited without exception.

⏳ Time Frame

One-Year Claim Policy

Students have exactly one calendar year from the award banquet date to officially claim their scholarship capital allocations.
