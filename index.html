Who We Are

Empowering Niagara Falls Students Since 1957

The LaSalle Educational Club Inc. is a Niagara Falls community organization dedicated to providing scholarship funding and recognition to graduating Niagara Falls High School students. We believe that Education Is Power — and that every deserving student deserves a path forward.

What began as a small social gathering of 25 mothers in 1957 has grown into one of the Niagara Falls area's most beloved scholarship organizations, bringing together more than 200 community members, parents, educators, and city representatives at our annual Black Scholarship & Award Banquet each year.

Our club funds scholarships through the proceeds of our Annual Scholarship & Award Dinner Banquet and community partnerships. We also honor outstanding community members and educators who embody our shared values.

College Scholarships

We provide need- and merit-based scholarships to first-year college, vocational, trade school, and military-bound graduates of Niagara Falls High School.

Community Recognition

Each year we honor outstanding community members with our Humanitarian, Community Service, and Educator Awards for their contributions to Niagara Falls.

Community Support

Beyond scholarships, we donate to area churches, the Negro College Fund, provide Pre-Thanksgiving dinners for seniors, and offer emergency financial support to families in need.

Family Empowerment

Rooted in the legacy of founder Lexie M. Sanders, the club has always believed in empowering families to be independent, resourceful, and educated.

Our Story

A Legacy Built on Love for Community

1957
Founder Lexie M. Sanders recruited 25 mothers and organized the Ever-Ready Mothers Club at the Griffon Manor Housing Complex. The club's goal: provide healthy social experiences for families and wholesome childhood experiences for children.
1967
The club gave its first Awards Banquet, honoring graduates of LaSalle Senior High School who lived in the Griffon Manor Housing Complex — a tradition that continues to this day.
1973
The club renamed itself the LaSalle Social Club and expanded its community efforts. That same year, the estate of Cleon Wilkins — an African American who believed "education was prosperity" — donated $634.00 through the Niagara Falls Board of Education.
1979
The first named scholarship — the Rev. James M. Bradley Scholarship for $300.00 — was awarded to Deborah Cooper of LaSalle Senior High School, commemorating the founder of Mt. Erie Missionary Baptist Church.
1989
The organization officially renamed itself the LaSalle Educational Club Inc. to better reflect its community-wide mission and educational focus.
1992–1997
The club expanded its charitable work beyond scholarships — running medical benefit campaigns for community members in need, raising thousands of dollars for families facing hardship.
Today
The Annual Scholarship Banquet has grown from 30 attendees to more than 200 people. To date, the club has awarded 574 scholarships totaling over $153,822, with 1,363 students participating in the scholarship program through the Niagara Falls City School District.

Community Leaders We Celebrate

Humanitarian Award
Yvonne Alston Taylor
A lifelong healthcare worker and dedicated community servant, Ms. Taylor has served as a Health Care Aide, Senior Companion, and active leader in her church's Mission Board, Usher Board, and Women's Fellowship at New Hope Baptist Church of Niagara Falls.
Community Service Award
Dorothy West
Born and raised in Niagara Falls, Dorothy has dedicated her life to community involvement — serving as Parent President of LaSalle Preparatory School, member of the Exchange Club, Kiwanis Club, Niagara County Peacemakers, Lion Club, and Niagara Gospel Mission.
Educator Award
Philip Mohr
An NFHS Class of 2004 alumnus and special education teacher at Cataract Elementary School, Mr. Mohr dedicates his days to students with communication and cognitive differences. He holds a Master of Science in Special Education from Canisius College and serves as President of the Niagara PAL Board of Directors.
